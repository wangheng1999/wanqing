This project was inspired by the Jiangnan gardens and Taihu stone elements of my hometown, Jiangsu, China. After conducting thorough research on the philosophy of Laozi and Jean Baudrillard's "Simulacra and Simulation" theory, and conducting extensive on-site investigations, I used the negative space of Taihu stone and abstractized garden elements to create a web interaction design using Figma, showcasing the intertwining of "form and emptiness," while guiding viewers to contemplate the concepts of "fluid space" and "empathy." The project aims to demonstrate the fluidity and dynamic equilibrium of the Taoist principles through the display of the interplay between "form" and "emptiness."
